## Method

### Braised beef

1. Preheat the oven to 140°C.

2. Brown the beef cheeks in a hot pan on all sides. Remove and set aside.

3. Add the carrots, celery, onion and half garlic bulb to the pan and cook until softened. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.

4. Pour in the red wine and beef stock. Season, then return the beef cheeks to the pan.

5. Cover and cook in the oven at 140°C for 2–3 hours until the beef is tender and pulls apart easily.

6. Remove the beef and shred. Strain the braising liquid through a colander and reserve.

### Haché parmentier

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.

2. Soften the 2 onions in a pan for 5–10 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another 5 minutes.

3. Add the shredded beef and the strained braising liquid. Thicken with the 65g butter and 65g flour, stirring until the sauce comes together. Check seasoning.

4. Boil the potatoes until soft. Drain, then mash with 75g butter, milk and seasoning.

5. Spoon the beef mixture into a baking dish. Top with the mash and spread evenly, then scatter over the grated emmental.

6. Bake at 200°C for 20 minutes until the top is crispy and golden.

## Serving suggestions

Serve warm with a light salad on the side and a glass of the leftover red wine.
